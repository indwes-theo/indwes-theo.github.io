Professional Bio

I am Theodore Yumba, an international graduate student at Indiana Wesleyan University pursuing a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence with a Data Analysis specialization. My academic background combines computer science, business management and finance, and my professional work as a software engineer has shaped my interest in building systems that solve practical problems.

Coming from a Congolese and Belgian background, I approach technology and analytics through a global lens, especially when thinking about access, infrastructure, business value and ethical decision-making. My goal is to build a career designing AI and data products that widen access to services in markets like my home region, and this portfolio documents that journey one artifact at a time.

The projects here share one thread: technology only counts when it works for the people actually using it.

Value Proposition & Audience

My value proposition: I help organizations build AI and data products that survive contact with the real world. I bring an engineer’s discipline for testing and guardrails, a business lens for value and cost, and a cross-cultural perspective shaped by living and working across the DR Congo, Belgium and the United States.

Engineer’s discipline

Success criteria defined before building. Adversarial testing designed to cause failure. Critical behavior pinned in written rules, never left to chance.

Business lens

Undergraduate roots in business management and finance. Every technical choice framed in terms of customer trust, cost, and operational reality.

Cross-cultural perspective

Products designed for feature phones and mobile money as first-class citizens, not afterthoughts. Local knowledge treated as a form of technical review.

Who this portfolio is for: hiring managers and teams building AI, data and digital products, particularly where fintech, e-commerce and emerging markets meet, and collaborators interested in responsible, accessible AI. Each artifact includes a plain-language walkthrough for non-technical reviewers and, where possible, a live link so you can test the work yourself.
